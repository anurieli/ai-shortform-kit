## Retention Principles (Non-Negotiable)

These rules are encoded in every template and enforced during self-edit:

### 1-Second Rule
The first FRAME must stop the scroll. Not the first 3 seconds — the first frame. Before audio even registers, the visual (face, text overlay, or unexpected image) must interrupt the scrolling motion.

### 3-Second Gate
Platforms measure 3-second retention. Your hook must create a reason to stay by second 3. If 50% of viewers leave before second 3, the video is dead algorithmically.

### 7-Second Cliff
The biggest drop-off point across all platforms. Something must CHANGE at 7-8 seconds — a camera angle shift, new information, energy change, or visual element. This is where commitment happens: viewers who pass 7 seconds are 60-80% likely to watch the majority.

### Information Density > Length
30 seconds with 6 ideas outperforms 60 seconds with 3 ideas. Every second must carry payload. If you can cut 10 seconds without losing value, cut them.

### Loop Effect
Videos that seamlessly connect the last 1-2 seconds back to the first 1-2 seconds create automatic replays. The algorithm counts replays as fresh watch-throughs. Techniques: end on a word that leads into the opening, visual that connects end-to-start, "Wait, did you catch that?" prompts.

### Pattern Interrupt Cadence
Every 8-12 seconds, something must change:
- Camera angle change (strongest)
- B-roll cutaway
- Text overlay appearing/changing
- Zoom in or out
- Sound effect or music shift
- Energy/tone shift

If nothing changes visually for more than 12 seconds, you lose viewers regardless of content quality.

### Save vs. Share Optimization
Know which metric you're optimizing:
- **Saves:** tutorials, listicles, frameworks, how-tos → CTA: "Save this for later"
- **Shares:** relatable humor, hot takes, identity content → viewers tag friends
- **Comments:** controversial takes, questions, "tell me yours" prompts → CTA drives discussion

## Platform Conventions

### TikTok
- **Algorithm:** Content-graph based. Follower count is nearly irrelevant for initial distribution. Every video is tested with 200-500 users first.
- **Sweet spot:** 21-34 seconds for virality; 60-90 seconds for depth
- **Culture:** Raw, authentic, trend-driven. Over-produced content can hurt.
- **Audio:** Trending sounds boost discovery significantly. Use TikTok's native sound library.
- **Posting:** 1-3x daily for growth accounts. Tuesday-Thursday, 10am-12pm and 7pm-9pm local time.
- **Unique feature:** Stitch and Duet create built-in collaboration/reaction mechanics.

### Instagram Reels
- **Algorithm:** Hybrid social-graph + content-graph. Accounts the viewer already interacts with get priority.
- **Sweet spot:** 15-30 seconds for reach; 30-60 seconds for engagement depth
- **Culture:** Slightly more polished than TikTok. Original content strongly preferred — watermarked reposts are penalized.
- **Audio:** Use Instagram's native music library for preferential treatment.
- **Posting:** 4-7 Reels per week. Monday-Wednesday, 11am-1pm and 7pm-9pm.
- **Unique feature:** Saves are weighted very heavily in the algorithm.

### YouTube Shorts
- **Algorithm:** Satisfaction-based, backed by YouTube's mature recommendation engine. Channel authority helps.
- **Sweet spot:** 30-58 seconds. YouTube expanded to 3 minutes but sub-60s gets preferential Shorts shelf placement.
- **Culture:** More educational and intent-driven than TikTok/Reels. Slightly older audience.
- **Shelf life:** Longest of all platforms — a Short can gain traction weeks or months after posting.
- **Posting:** 3-5 per week minimum. Thursday-Saturday, 12pm-3pm and 5pm-8pm.
- **Unique feature:** Drives long-form subscribers. Shorts can funnel viewers to full YouTube videos.

## Audio/Sound Trend Handling

- **Trending audio boosts discovery** on TikTok — the algorithm surfaces content using trending sounds
- **For talking-head scripts:** Subtle background music at 10-15% volume adds energy without distracting
- **Sound effects at pattern interrupts** (whoosh, pop, ding) reinforce visual cuts
- **Describe sounds textually in scripts:** "[SOUND: upbeat lo-fi beat, 80bpm]" or "[SOUND: record scratch transition]"
- **Never rely solely on audio** — 80%+ of viewers browse with sound off initially

---

## Caption/Subtitle Best Practices

- **Always include captions.** 80-85% of social media video is watched without sound initially.
- **Style:** 2-3 words per caption chunk, appearing in sync with speech, centered on screen
- **Designed captions > auto-captions:** Animated, styled captions (CapCut style) outperform platform auto-captions
- **Highlight key words:** Change color or size of important words within caption sequences
- **Safe zones:** Keep captions away from bottom 20% (platform UI) and top 15% (username overlay)

## Cross-Posting Adaptation

### TikTok → Reels
- Remove TikTok watermark (Instagram penalizes it)
- Re-export clean version at 1080x1920
- Adjust caption positioning for Instagram's UI elements
- Use Instagram's native music library instead of TikTok sounds

### TikTok → Shorts
- YouTube's safe zone for text is different — keep text more centered
- Slightly more educational framing (YouTube audience is more intent-driven)
- Lighter text overlay density
- Description and tags matter more on YouTube

### Universal Rendering
- Resolution: 1080x1920 (9:16)
- Frame rate: 30fps minimum
- Keep critical text/visuals within center 80% of frame
- Test on multiple devices before publishing

## Common Mistakes the System Must Prevent

1. **Starting with "Hey guys"** or any throat-clearing. First word = first value.
2. **Using first 5 seconds for context** instead of hook. Hook first, context second.
3. **Writing for reading instead of speaking.** Complex sentences, subordinate clauses, academic language — all death for spoken content.
4. **Generic CTA** that doesn't match the content's energy. "Follow for more" is invisible.
5. **Scripts too long for the format.** 200 words crammed into 30 seconds = rushed and unnatural.
6. **Same template repeatedly.** Variety prevents audience fatigue.
7. **Hooks that promise but don't deliver.** Clickbait destroys creator credibility.
8. **No pattern interrupts.** Static talking head for 60 seconds hemorrhages viewers after 10 seconds.
9. **No captions.** Losing 80%+ of initial viewers.
10. **Over-polishing.** Excessive production value can hurt on TikTok/Reels where authenticity signals relatability.
