# Writing Starter — Get Your First Samples

This system writes video scripts in **your** voice. To do that, it needs to study how you actually talk and think. No existing scripts or writing? No problem — here's how to get started.

## Why samples matter

Claude doesn't just follow instructions like "write in an energetic tone." It studies the specific patterns in YOUR communication — your vocabulary, your pacing, your humor, how you explain things, how you open and close. The more real writing it has, the more the scripts sound like you on camera.

Without samples, Claude can still interview you about your style, but the results won't be as accurate. Even 3-5 short pieces make a huge difference.

## What counts as a sample

Any writing in your natural voice works:
- Tweets or threads you've written
- Blog posts or articles
- Emails where you were being yourself (not corporate-formal)
- Social media captions
- Messages where you explained something you're passionate about
- Even voice memos you've transcribed

The key is that it sounds like **you**, not like you trying to sound professional.

## Quick-start: write 5 pieces right now

Don't overthink these. Write them the way you'd actually talk to someone. Spend 5-10 minutes each. They don't need to be published — they just need to sound like you.

**Piece 1 — Explain what you do**
Imagine someone asks "so what do you do?" Write your answer the way you'd actually say it out loud — not the polished version.

**Piece 2 — A strong opinion**
What's something you believe about your niche that most people get wrong? Write it with conviction, the way you'd rant about it to a friend.

**Piece 3 — Teach something in a few paragraphs**
Pick one thing you know well. Explain it as if someone has zero context. Just talk through it naturally.

**Piece 4 — Tell a quick story**
Think of something that happened to you recently — a conversation, a win, a mistake. Tell the story the way you'd tell a friend.

**Piece 5 — React to something**
Find a post or article in your niche. Write your reaction — what you agree with, what you'd push back on, what they missed.

## What to do with them

1. Save your writing as text files, or just paste them directly into Claude when asked
2. Open your ai-shortform-kit folder in Claude
3. When Claude asks for samples during setup, share them
4. Claude will analyze your voice and build your persona profile

## Tips

- Write like you talk. Don't try to sound polished. The whole point is capturing YOUR natural voice.
- Longer is better than shorter. A few paragraphs give Claude more signal than a couple sentences.
- Variety helps. Different topics and moods help Claude capture your full range.
