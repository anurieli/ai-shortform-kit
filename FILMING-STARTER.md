# Filming Starter — Get Your First Samples

This system writes video scripts in **your** on-camera voice. To do that, it needs to study how you actually talk and present. No existing videos? No problem — here's how to get started.

## Why samples matter

Claude doesn't just follow instructions like "write in an energetic tone." It studies the specific patterns in YOUR delivery — your energy, your pacing, how you open videos, your humor, your hand gestures, your vocabulary. The more real video it can study, the more the scripts sound like you on camera.

Without samples, Claude can still interview you about your style, but the results won't be as accurate. Even 3-5 short clips make a huge difference.

## Quick-start: record 5 clips right now

Grab your phone. No editing needed. No fancy setup. Just you talking to the camera for 30-60 seconds each. These don't need to be posted — they just need to sound like you.

**Clip 1 — Explain what you do**
Imagine someone at a party asks "so what do you do?" Record your answer the way you'd actually say it.

**Clip 2 — A strong opinion**
What's something you believe about your niche that most people get wrong? Say it with conviction.

**Clip 3 — Teach something in 60 seconds**
Pick one thing you know well. Explain it as if someone has zero context. Just talk through it naturally.

**Clip 4 — Tell a quick story**
Think of something that happened to you recently — a conversation, a win, a mistake. Tell the story the way you'd tell a friend.

**Clip 5 — React to something**
Find a post, video, or article in your niche. Record yourself reacting to it — what you agree with, what you'd push back on.

## What to do with them

1. Transcribe them (or just describe what you said — Claude can work with rough transcripts)
2. Drop the transcripts into `identity/video-samples/` or paste them directly into Claude
3. When Claude asks for video samples during setup, share them
4. Claude will analyze your persona across 8 dimensions and build your profile

## Tips

- Talk like yourself. Don't perform. The whole point is capturing YOUR natural on-camera presence.
- Keep them casual. Rough phone recordings are better than overly produced takes.
- Variety helps. Different topics and energy levels help Claude capture your full range.
- Already have videos? Even old ones work. Links, transcripts, or descriptions all help.
